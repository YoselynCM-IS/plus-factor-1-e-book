Family ties

 

Is your family large or small?

 

Where do you come from?

 

What is your place in your family?

 

Do you have a family tree?

 

In this unit you will

 

•
Learn the use of  
There is 
,
there are

 

•
Learn vocabulary about Family members

 

•
Use adjectives to describe people

 

•
Use of Possessive adjectives, ‘s possession.

 

•
Use Simple Present affirmative, negative and interrogative

 

•
Learn most used verbs

 

Learning outcome

 

•
Students use the affirmative form of verb  
to be 
in simple present as well as personal

 

pronouns to provide their own information in written form.

 

•
Students understand the use of verb  
to be 
in simple present in affirmative form and personal

 

pronouns to describe people.

 

•
Students use vocabulary related to family members.

 

•
Students use possessive adjectives to talk about their relationship with family members.

 

•
Students use some verbs in simple present to talk about their daily activities and other

 

people's routines.

 

•
Students draw a family tree and include basic information about themselves and two relatives

 

(name, age, occupation, and physical appearance).

 

•
Students include in their family tree their daily routines by using some verbs and frequency adverbs.

 

•
Students write about the daily routine of two more family members, using simple present verbs.

 

Learning evidence

 

•
A family tree providing students description and daily routines and the descriptions and daily

 

routines of two members of their families
