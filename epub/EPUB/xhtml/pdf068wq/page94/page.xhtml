Learn the rules for Comparatives and Superlatives

 

There are three degrees of comparison: Positive – Comparative – Superlative

 

We use the comparative degree when we compare two people, animals or things:

 

Robert is  
taller than 
Paul. Tina is  
more intelligent than 
Rita.

 

We use superlative degree when we compare one person, animal or thing with several others

 

of the same kind:

 

Hellen is the  
fastest 
worker in the office. This book is the  
most interesting 
of all.

 

One-syllable adjectives and some two syllable adjectives: we form the comparative by adding

 

–er
and the word  
than 
, and the superlative degree by adding  
–est  
to the positive form:

 

An elephant is  
heavier than  
a hippo. This box is the  
heaviest of 
all.

 

Adjectives with three or more syllables and some two-syllable adjectives: we form their

 

comparative with more and their superlative degree with most + the positive form.

 

My tablet is  
more expensive than 
his tablet. Alaska is  
the coldest  
area in the continent.

 

Adjectives with one syllable + one consonant double the final consonant and add  
–er. 

 

My friend is  
funnier than  
your friend.

 

Adjectives ending in consonant and + y change the –y to –i before  
–er
:

 

pretty – prettier – prettiest 
 
noisy – noisier - noisiest

 

Adjectives ending in  
–e
take only  
–r
/
–st

 

large – larger - largest

 

Irregular Comparatives and Superlatives

 

Bad  
worse than  
the worst of

 

Good  
better than  
the best of

 

Far  
farther/further than  
the farthest/ furthest of

 

Little  
less than  
the least of

 

Much/many  
more than  
the most of
