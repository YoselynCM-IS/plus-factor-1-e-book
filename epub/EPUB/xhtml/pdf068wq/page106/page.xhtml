Landmarks

 

everywhere

 

Is there a landmark like a monument, ruin, or historical

 

building in your community?

 

How many landmarks do you know in your country?

 

Have you visited them?

 

In this unit you will

 

•
Ask information about places.  
•
Review of indefinite articles.

 

•
Use prepositions of movement. 
•
Vocabulary about places.

 

•
Review of  
there is 
/
there are 
.

 

Learning outcome

 

•
Students read a tourist guide of a city and recognize the most important places to ask

 

information about them.

 

•
Students use prepositions of movement to express how to get to some places.

 

•
Students use  
there is 
and  
there are 
to talk about public spaces.

 

•
Students use indefinite articles.

 

•
Students use questions and directions to get to different public spaces.

 

•
Students use vocabulary about the name of different places.

 

•
Students use  
Wh–  
questions to ask for directions and how to get to a place.

 

•
Students write, perform and record a sketch in which they ask for eight different

 

public avenues and get directions to arrive to three of those places.

 

Learning evidence

 

•
A video showing people asking for eight different places in a city and asking for

 

directions to get to those places.
